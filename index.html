Bio

I'm currently a second-year Ph.D. student in the Urban Infrastructure Lab at the Urban Design & Planning Interdisciplinary Ph.D. Program at the University of Washington. I am fortunate to be advised by Dr. Jan Whittington and Dr. Qing Shen.

I am interested in physical, cultural, and institutional spaces within city boundaries and my current research interests are in the ethical discussions about AI applications in urban planning, especially in the era of climate change. I'm doing theoretical and empirical work on energy, infrastructure planning, climate impact, and artificial intelligence as well as developing tools to assist cities in making climate-related decisions. Check my CV here.

My master's and doctoral coursework plans are a combination of urban planning, public policy, and data science resources at the University of Washington. Please check my full coursework list here to get some inspirations. Also, you can refer to the programs and courses recommended by the Urban Infrastructure Lab. Hope this information is helpful to you.
